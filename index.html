Common questions

Straight answers in plain English—if something’s missing, reach out on LinkedIn or GitHub.

How do you usually price or scope a project?

It depends on the goal: a small landing page, a Unity prototype, or a multi-week product build all need different plans. I typically start with a short call to clarify outcomes, timeline, and constraints, then propose either a fixed milestone quote or weekly engagement for ongoing work. You’ll always get a written summary before any code starts.

Do you build websites, games, or both?

Both. On the web I use PHP, Node, and classic HTML/CSS/JS (and modern tooling where it fits). For interactive and game-style work I use Unity and C#. If you’re unsure which stack fits your idea, describe the experience you want for users and I’ll recommend a sensible path.

How do you use AI in your development workflow?

I treat AI as an accelerator—not a substitute for judgment. Tools like Cursor, Copilot, and chat assistants help with boilerplate, exploration, and documentation, while reviews, tests, and architecture stay human-led. That keeps shipping fast without sacrificing maintainability.

Can you collaborate with an existing team or repo?

Yes. I’m used to GitHub workflows, branches, and code review. I can plug into your backlog, follow your style guide, and document what I change so the team can own it long term.

What’s the best way to contact you?

For professional inquiries, LinkedIn is ideal. You can also open a discussion on GitHub, or reach me on X (Twitter), YouTube, or TikTok for community and updates—links are in the footer. I aim to reply to serious project messages within a couple of business days.
